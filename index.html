VEHICLES

Electronic Vehicles (EVs) are automobiles that are powered by electricity rather than traditional internal combustion engines fueled by petroleum or diesel. These vehicles utilize electric motors and rechargeable batteries to convert electrical energy into the mechanical energy required for propulsion.Unlike traditional vehicles that rely on internal combustion engines powered by petroleum or diesel, EVs are powered by electricity stored in batteries. These batteries can be charged through a variety of methods, such as plugging into an electric power source or utilizing regenerative braking. EVs are known for their environmental benefits, as they produce zero tailpipe emissions and contribute to reducing dependence on fossil fuels. They come in various forms, including all-electric vehicles and plug-in hybrid vehicles.

TYPES OF EV

BATTERY ELECTRIC VHICLES (BEVs)

Battery Electric Vehicles are fully electric vehicles that are powered solely by electricity. They have no internal combustion engine and rely on rechargeable batteries to store and provide energy for the electric motor.

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles combine an electric motor and an internal combustion engine (usually gasoline) and can operate in both electric-only and hybrid modes. They can be charged via an electrical outlet.
